NOTE: SIGN CONVENTION FOR THE THIRD LABOR-FORCE RECODING (Table 4, col. 3)
============================================================================

ISSUE
-----
child_test.Rmd's labor-force reproduction table currently prints the RAW
2SLS/kappa coefficient for all three labor recodings. For the first two
columns (workedm, workedm_21 = workedm+1) this is correct and matches
Słoczyński, Uysal & Wooldridge (SUW 2025), Table 4, since both are pure
translations of the same underlying variable and normalized estimators are
translation invariant by construction.

For the third column (workedm_01 = 1 - workedm, i.e. "1 if did not work, 0
otherwise"), the raw coefficient is NOT what SUW's Table 4 reports. This
recoding is a genuine reflection of the outcome (Y' = 1 - Y, not just a
shift), and for any linear-in-Y estimator (2SLS, kappa weighting, OLS —
no exceptions, this is elementary regression algebra) the raw coefficient on
D must flip sign relative to column 1. Our own subsample output already
shows this correctly: labor table gives -0.096 (col 1) and +0.096 (col 3),
a clean, mathematically required sign flip.

SUW's own Table 4 note resolves why their printed column 3 does NOT show a
sign flip (all three labor columns print as -0.117 in the published table).
Direct quote from the paper (Table 4 note, JBES 2025, p. 173-174; also in
project file Abadie_s_Kappa_and_Weighting_Estimators_of_the_Local_Average_
Treatment_Effect1.pdf):

  "In the case of labor force participation, we also recode the outcome as
  2 if worked for pay and 1 otherwise; and as 0 if worked for pay and 1
  otherwise. In the latter case, we report the additive inverse of each
  estimate."

So: SUW deliberately negate the raw regression coefficient before printing
column 3, purely as a presentational device so every normalized row reads
identically across all three columns at a glance. This is documented,
intentional, and NOT a property the estimator produces on its own — it is
a manual sign flip applied by the authors after estimation.

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR OUR CODE
-----------------------------
- Our current raw-coefficient output (sign flips in col. 3) is mathematically
  correct and not a bug.
- It does not yet visually match SUW Table 4's layout, because we have not
  applied their documented additive-inverse convention.
