AE98 (ANGRIST & EVANS 1998) CHILDBEARING APPLICATION — STATUS
================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Translation/scale-invariance study of LATE estimators (2SLS, kappa-weighting,
DML) applied to Angrist & Evans (1998): does the estimated effect of
childbearing (D=morekids, Z=samesex) on labor supply change under
mathematically irrelevant recodings of the outcome (adding a constant,
rescaling units, flipping the reference category)? This mirrors a parallel
analysis already done for Card (1995, schooling/wages) — same estimators,
same diagnostics, same translation-invariance logic — adapted to AE98's own
structure. Two things make AE98 structurally different from Card and drive
most of the design choices below: (1) AE98 needs two different
outcome-specific samples, not one; (2) the full AE98 samples are too large
(394,840 / 220,502 rows) for the smoother-matrix/weight diagnostics, so
everything runs on small stratified subsamples instead.

FILES AND HOW THEY FIT TOGETHER
--------------------------------
- ae98_make_balanced_subsample_submission.Rmd
  Builds two 3,000-row stratified diagnostic subsamples from the full AE98
  data and saves them to ae98_sub.rds:
    ae98_labor_sub  -> stratified on (samesex, morekids, workedm, race, age),
                       for the labor-force-participation outcome (workedm).
    ae98_income_sub -> stratified on (samesex, morekids, income decile, race,
                       age), for the log-income outcome (lincomem), drawn
                       from the positive-income parent population.
  ae98_sub.rds ALSO contains a generic key `ae98_sub`, which is just an
  alias for `ae98_labor_sub` left over from an early draft. IGNORE IT — see
  Hard Rules.

- child_test.Rmd
  Main analysis notebook. Loads ae98_sub.rds, then:
    Section 1: SUW Table 4 reproduction, split into a labor tab (3 outcome
      recodings) and an income tab (4 unit recodings), since the two
      outcome families use different samples and the full published table
      can't be matched at N=3,000 anyway. 2SLS + 6 kappa estimators per tab.
    Section 2: DML via dml_with_smoother() (grf honest forest), fit BOTH
      untuned (tune.parameters="none") and tuned (tune.parameters="all"),
      both samples -- PLR-IV and Wald-AIPW, with identity checks.
    Section 3: mlr3-based learner comparison (LATE/Wald-AIPW-type estimate).
      Linear+logit and Ranger are fit fresh here. XGBoost is NOT fit here --
      it is IMPORTED from ae98_xgb_tuning_export.rds (see below and Hard
      Rules). Combined comparison table includes kappa reference rows.
    Section 4: weight diagnostics -- DML vs. kappa, learners vs. kappa,
      weight-statistics bridge (Knaus summary stats + ESS Kish/modified),
      ESS overview table + dumbbell plot. Both samples.
    Section 5: Love plots -- DML vs. kappa (10 panels/sample), learners vs.
      kappa (6 panels/sample). Both samples.

- child_xgb_tuning.Rmd
  Separate XGBoost tuning appendix, scoped to ONE binary outcome (workedm,
  labor sample) and ONE continuous outcome (lincomem, income sample) only --
  not every recoding. Fits untuned XGBoost vs. tuned XGBoost
  (tune_on_folds=TRUE, i.e. hyperparameters are re-selected separately
  inside each of the 5 outer cross-fitting folds via 3-fold inner CV /
  random search on nuisance-prediction loss). Produces: estimate comparison,
  nuisance-loss comparison, a descriptive fold-median hyperparameter
  summary, weight/ESS diagnostics with identity checks, and an export chunk
  that saves everything (including the raw omega weight vectors and
  identity-check flags) to ae98_xgb_tuning_export.rds. This is the only
  bridge between the two files -- child_test.Rmd reads this RDS, it never
  re-tunes or re-fits XGBoost itself.

- ae98_sign_convention_note.txt
  Standalone note on the SUW Table 4 sign-flip issue (see Hard Rules, item 6).

- child_translation_invariance_method_a.Rmd
  Method-A rerun check: two workflows only (Labor: workedm vs. workedm+1, k=1;
  Income: lincomem vs. lincomem+log(100), k=log(100)) -- NOT every SUW
  recoding. workedm_01=1-workedm excluded (reflection, not a translation).
  Sections: (1) kappa, all 6 estimators, cheap; (2) DML headline via
  dml_with_smoother, TUNED honest forest only; (3a) linear+logit (6-cov) and
  Ranger (7-cov), fresh refit both outcomes; (3b) XGBoost, gated behind
  RUN_XGB / RUN_XGB_LABOR_ONLY switches (default OFF) -- does a FULL
  tune_on_folds=TRUE retune+refit on both the original and shifted outcome
  each time; never reuses ae98_xgb_tuning_export.rds for this (that export
  is only valid for descriptive tables and the Method-B frozen-weight
  check, not for a Method-A rerun on a different Y). Loads the exact same
  ae98_labor_sub/ae98_income_sub, no resampling.

3. HARD RULES / DO NOT DO
---------------------------
1. Never use the full parent AE98 samples (394,840 labor / 220,502 income)
   in child_test.Rmd or child_xgb_tuning.Rmd. Only the two 3,000-row
   diagnostic subsamples.
2. Never load data via the generic `ae98_sub` key in ae98_sub.rds -- it is
   an alias for ae98_labor_sub left over from an early draft. Always load
   `ae98_labor_sub` and `ae98_income_sub` explicitly.
3. Never mix labor-sample rows into the income analysis, or vice versa.
4. Never drop `sex2nd` for kappa, Ranger, XGBoost, or the grf honest forest.
   It is dropped ONLY for the linear+logit learner's design matrix, because
   that is the one arm-specific PARAMETRIC nuisance model where sexk/sex2nd
   are exactly collinear within a fixed Z arm (Z=1{sexk==sex2nd} forces
   sexk=sex2nd when Z=1 and sexk=1-sex2nd when Z=0) and a design-matrix
   inversion breaks. Tree-based learners and pooled propensity models are
   unaffected and keep all 7 covariates.
5. Never tune any nuisance learner (in child_xgb_tuning.Rmd or anywhere
   else) by looking at the treatment-effect estimate. Tuning objective is
   nuisance-prediction loss (RMSE / log-loss) only.
6. child_test.Rmd must NEVER take child_xgb_tuning.Rmd's median/fold-summary
   hyperparameter table and plug those values into a fresh DoubleMLIIVM fit.
   tune_on_folds=TRUE produces fold-specific hyperparameters -- there is no
   single global recipe to reuse. Always import the already-fitted results
   from ae98_xgb_tuning_export.rds instead.
7. Never set.seed() once at the top of a script and assume it covers every
   later stochastic call. R has one global RNG stream; reseed immediately
   before every dml_with_smoother() / DoubleMLIIVM fit / tuning call that
   needs to be reproducible or compared against a counterpart run.
8. SUW's Table 4 additive-inverse sign flip (labor's "did not work" column)
   is a DISPLAY-ONLY convention for that one reproduction-table column.
   Never apply it in Section 2 DML, Section 3 learner comparison, or the
   future translation-invariance file.
9. For the future translation-invariance file: never use
   workedm_01 = 1-workedm as a translation-invariance test -- it is a
   reflection, not a pure translation, and does not isolate the same
   question. Use workedm vs. workedm+1 (k=1) and lincomem vs.
   lincomem+log(100) (k=log(100)). The sample (exact rows) must be held
   fixed across the original and shifted fits -- no resampling between them.
